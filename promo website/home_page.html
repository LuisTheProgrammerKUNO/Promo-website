Welcome to Marballin!

What is Marballin?

A marble-based roguelike game, inspired by the recent success of games like Balatro, which gained attention after winning multiple awards, including nominations at Geoff Keighley's 10th annual Game Awards. Drawing inspiration from the deck-building mechanics of games like Slay the Spire and Balatro, this project aims to integrate mathematics as a core gameplay element, enhancing educational value while maintaining the strategic depth typical of roguelikes.



Key elements of the game design include:


1. Core Roguelike Features:

Permadeath: Players must restart from scratch after three failed attempts, encouraging strategic thinking and experimentation.

Incremental Difficulty: Progressively tougher stages that require card upgrades to advance.

Procedural Generation: Randomized seeds ensure unique playthroughs, enhancing replayability.


2. Gameplay Modes:

Singleplayer: Endless runs with a focus on score progression, featuring offline leaderboards to track high scores.

Multiplayer (Battle Royale): A competitive mode for 2 to 10 players, inspired by Teamfight Tactics, where players survive through stages and duels until only one remains.

The game aims to blend the educational benefits of mathematics with the challenge and replayability of roguelike mechanics, targeting both single-player enthusiasts and competitive multiplayer audiences.



What is the objective of Marballin?

The main objective of this study is to design, develop, and implement Marballin , a math-based marble roguelite strategy game that integrates algebraic concepts into physics-based gameplay. The study specifically aims to:


1. Math Integration: Create a game that encourages players to apply mathematical concepts through experimental, physics-driven mechanics.

2. Familiar Genre Fusion: Blend mathematics with familiar roguelike and strategy game elements.

3. Educational Impact: Enhance mathematical awareness and knowledge in a fun, engaging manner.

4. Modern Game Features: Include scaling difficulties, multiplayer modes, online support, leaderboards, and a progression system for broader player engagement.
